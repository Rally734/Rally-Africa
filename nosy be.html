Views Of Nosy Be

About

location:

Diana Region, Northwest coast, Madagascar

Nosy Be is a small island located off the northwest coast of Madagascar. It is known for its stunning beaches, clear waters, and vibrant marine life, making it a popular destination for tourists and divers.

The island offers a range of activities for visitors, including snorkeling, scuba diving, fishing, and boat tours. Nosy Be is also home to several nature reserves and parks where visitors can explore the diverse flora and fauna of the region.

In addition to its natural beauty, Nosy Be offers a glimpse into Malagasy culture and traditions, with various local markets, restaurants, and cultural events that showcase the island's unique heritage. Visitors can also immerse themselves in the local culture by visiting traditional villages, trying local cuisine, and experiencing traditional music and dance performances.

Overall, Nosy Be is a tropical paradise with stunning landscapes, warm hospitality, and plenty of opportunities for adventure and relaxation. It's a destination that offers something for everyone, whether you're looking to unwind on the beach or explore the island's natural wonders.

Things to know before travelling to Nosy Be

This article is a tip of the iceberg but will equip you with the "Absolute Need to Knows" for Nosy Be.

How to get there?

The easiest way to get to Nosy Be is by flying into Fascene Airport, which is located on the island. There are regular flights to Fascene Airport from the cities of Antananarivo, Toliara, and Mahajanga in Madagascar.

Once you arrive at Fascene Airport, you can take a taxi or a bus to your final destination on the island. If you're coming from outside of Madagascar, you'll need to fly into Ivato International Airport in Antananarivo and then take a connecting flight to Fascene Airport.

After you've arrived at Fascene Airport, you'll need to make your way to your final destination on the island. The main towns on Nosy Be are Hell-Ville, Ambatoloaka, and Andilana. The best way to get around the island is by taxi or by renting a scooter.

If you want to explore the island on your own, we recommend renting a scooter. Scooters are relatively inexpensive to rent and give you the freedom to explore the island at your own pace.

Medical matters that affect your visit.

Medical matters that may affect your visit to Nosy Be, Madagascar, include:

Malaria

Nosy Be is a malaria-endemic area, so it's important to take prophylactic medication, use insect repellent, and sleep under mosquito nets to prevent infection.

Dengue Fever

Dengue fever is also present in Madagascar, including Nosy Be. Take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, such as using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants.

Travelers Diarrhea

Changes in diet and water quality can lead to traveler's diarrhea. Drink bottled water, avoid street food, and wash fruits and vegetables with safe water.

Sunburn and Heat-related illnesses

The tropical climate in Nosy Be can lead to sunburn and heat-related illnesses. Stay hydrated, use sun protection, and seek shade during peak sunlight hours.

Waterborne Diseases

Contaminated water sources may lead to diseases like cholera or typhoid. Drink bottled or purified water and avoid consuming uncooked foods washed in untreated water.

Wildlife Hazards

Nosy Be is known for its diverse wildlife, including marine animals that can cause injuries or infections. Be cautious when interacting with wildlife and seek medical attention if bitten or stung.

Limited Healthcare Facilities

Medical services in Nosy Be may be limited, so consider purchasing travel insurance that covers medical evacuation in case of serious illness or injury.

It's important to consult with a healthcare provider or travel medicine specialist before your trip to receive personalized advice based on your medical history and travel plans.

Cultural Ettiquettes at Nosy Be

When visiting Nosy Be, it's important to be aware of some cultural etiquettes. First, it's considered rude to touch someone's head, so avoid this gesture when interacting with locals. Second, you should always remove your shoes before entering a home, and you should never sit on a bed with shoes on.

Third, the right hand is considered the "clean" hand, and should be used for eating and greeting others. It's also important to dress modestly, especially in rural areas. Avoid wearing revealing clothing, and cover your shoulders and legs.

Language and communication at Nosy Be

In Nosy Be, the main mode of communication is spoken Malagasy. However, English is also widely spoken, especially in areas frequented by tourists. If you're planning on traveling to more remote areas, it may be helpful to learn a few phrases in Malagasy.

Additionally, some locals may speak French, as it is the official language of Madagascar. Internet access and cell phone coverage are available in most areas of Nosy Be, but may be limited in some rural areas.

Safety Precautions at Nosy Be

Nosy Be is considered a safe destination, but it's still important to take some basic precautions. Keep an eye on your personal belongings, especially in crowded areas. Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in isolated areas.

Additionally, keep an eye out for pickpockets and scammers, who may target tourists. If you take these basic precautions, you should have a safe and enjoyable trip to Nosy Be.

Vital Information on Money Matters

When it comes to money matters, the official currency of Madagascar is the ariary, but US dollars are also widely accepted in Nosy Be. However, it's a good idea to have small bills and coins in ariary to make small purchases. ATM machines are available in major towns, but they may not always be reliable.

Additionally, credit cards are accepted at most hotels and restaurants, but not all businesses accept them. Lastly, tipping is not expected, but it is appreciated.

Fun things to do at Nosy Be

Here are some fun things to do at Nosy Be, Madagascar:

Beach hopping

With its stunning white-sand beaches and crystal-clear waters, Nosy Be is perfect for beach lovers. You can explore different beaches such as Andilana Beach, Madirokely Beach, and Ambatoloaka Beach.

Snorkeling and Diving

Nosy Be offers some of the best snorkeling and diving opportunities in Madagascar. You can discover colorful coral reefs, marine life, and underwater caves in the Nosy Tanikely Marine Reserve or at nearby islands like Nosy Sakatia.

Whale watching

If you visit Nosy Be between July and September, you can go on a whale-watching excursion to see humpback whales as they migrate through the area. It's a spectacular sight to witness these majestic creatures up close.

Visit Nosy Komba

Take a boat trip to Nosy Komba, a nearby island known for its lemurs. You can interact with these friendly creatures in their natural habitat and also explore the island's lush forests and stunning views.

Explore Lekobe National Park

This protected rainforest reserve is home to various species of lemurs, chameleons, and unique flora. You can go on guided hikes through the park to appreciate Madagascar's rich biodiversity.

Try local cuisine

Nosy Be offers a variety of delicious dishes influenced by Malagasy, French, and Creole flavors. Don't miss trying local specialties like seafood dishes, vanilla-infused desserts, and refreshing fruit juices.

Water activities

In addition to snorkeling and diving, you can enjoy other water activities such as kayaking, windsurfing, or sailing around the pristine waters of Nosy Be. There are plenty of water sports opportunities to keep you entertained.

Visit Nosy Tanikely Marine Reserve

This marine reserve is a paradise for snorkelers and divers, with its clear waters teeming with marine life. You can explore vibrant coral reefs, encounter tropical fish, and relax on the beautiful beaches within the reserve.

Sunset cruises

End your day with a relaxing sunset cruise around Nosy Be. Enjoy breathtaking views of the sunset over the Indian Ocean while sipping on a tropical cocktail and soaking in the island's beauty.

These are just a few of the fun things you can do at Nosy Be, Madagascar. The island offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, wildlife encounters, water activities, and cultural experiences for an unforgettable vacation.

Who can travel to Nosy Be?

In general, anyone can travel to Nosy Be, but there are a few visa requirements to be aware of. Citizens of the United States, Canada, Australia, and most European countries do not need a visa for stays of up to 90 days. However, citizens of other countries may need to obtain a visa before traveling to Madagascar.

However, travelers with disabilities may encounter some challenges, as accessibility is limited in some areas. The sidewalks and streets in Nosy Be can be uneven and difficult to navigate for those with mobility issues. Additionally, not all businesses and accommodations are accessible for those with disabilities.

However, with proper planning, most travelers with disabilities should be able to enjoy their visit to Nosy Be.

Travel Documents

You will need a valid passport and visa to enter Madagascar. Make sure your passport is valid for at least six months after your travel date and check if you need to obtain a visa before traveling.

What time of the year is best to visit?

If you're looking for the best time to visit Nosy Be, the dry season (from May to October) is generally considered the best time. During this time, the weather is pleasant and there is less chance of rain.

However, the dry season is also the peak tourist season, so prices may be higher and accommodations may be more difficult to find. The wet season (from November to April) can be a good time to visit for those looking for lower prices and fewer crowds, but the weather can be hot and humid, and there is a higher chance of rain.

Packing essentials for your trip

Here is a list of packing essentials for a trip to Nosy Be, Madagascar:

Lightweight clothing

Pack light and breathable clothing suitable for the warm and humid weather in Nosy Be. Cotton clothes, shorts, t-shirts, and swimwear are recommended.

Sun protection

Don't forget to pack a wide-brimmed hat, sunglasses, and high SPF sunscreen to protect yourself from the strong sun rays in Madagascar.

Insect repellent

Given the tropical climate, it's important to pack insect repellent to ward off mosquitoes and other insects that may carry diseases.

Water bottle

Stay hydrated throughout your trip by carrying a reusable water bottle. It's also advisable to bring water purification tablets or a filtered water bottle for safe drinking water.

Comfortable footwear

Bring sturdy and comfortable shoes for exploring the island. Consider packing hiking sandals or water shoes for beach activities and excursions.

Travel adapter and charger

Ensure you have the necessary adapters to charge your electronics in Madagascar. It's also wise to bring a portable charger to keep your devices powered during your adventures.

Basic first aid kit

Pack a small first aid kit with essentials like bandaids, antiseptic cream, pain relievers, and any personal medications you may need.

Passport and travel documents

Make sure to carry your passport, tickets, travel insurance information, and any necessary visas in a secure and waterproof pouch.

Snorkeling gear

If you plan to enjoy the underwater beauty of Nosy Be, consider bringing your snorkeling gear such as a mask, snorkel, and fins.

Camera or smartphone

Capture the stunning landscapes and wildlife of Nosy Be by carrying a camera or smartphone with a waterproof case.

Remember to pack light to make your travels easier and more enjoyable. Additionally, check the weather forecast before your trip to ensure you pack appropriate clothing for your stay in Nosy Be.
