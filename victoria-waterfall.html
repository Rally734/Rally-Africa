Town of Victoria Falls, Zimbabwe

Victoria Falls, located in Zimbabwe and Zambia, is one of the most spectacular waterfalls in the world. It is also known as Mosi-oa-Tunya, which means "The Smoke That Thunders" in the local indigenous language.

With a width of 1,708 meters (5,604 feet) and a height of 108 meters (354 feet), Victoria Falls is considered the largest waterfall in the world based on its combined width and height.

The waterfall is created by the Zambezi River, which plunges into a deep gorge, forming a breathtaking cascade of water. The mist and spray from the falls can be seen and felt from miles away, giving the area a mystical and ethereal atmosphere.

Visitors to Victoria Falls can enjoy a range of activities, including guided tours, boat cruises, helicopter rides, and even bungee jumping for the thrill-seekers. The surrounding area is also home to diverse wildlife, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike.

Victoria Falls is not only a natural wonder but also holds cultural and spiritual significance for the local communities, adding to its allure and mystique. It is truly a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the awe-inspiring power and beauty of nature.

Things to know before travelling to victoria waterfalls

This article is a tip of the iceberg but will equip you with the "Absolute Need to Knows" for Victoria falls.

How to get there?

There are a few ways to get to Victoria Falls, which is located on the border of Zimbabwe and Zambia. The most common way to get there is to fly into Victoria Falls Airport, which is located in Zimbabwe. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to the falls.

You can also fly into Livingstone Airport in Zambia, which is about 20 kilometers from the falls. From Livingstone, you can take a taxi or bus to the falls as well. There are also a few overland tour companies that offer trips to Victoria Falls.

About the weather

Victoria Falls is located in a tropical climate, so the weather can be quite hot and humid. The temperature can reach up to 40 degrees Celsius in the summer months, and there is often heavy rainfall.

The rainy season is from November to March, and the dry season is from May to October. During the dry season, the water level of the falls is lower, but it is still an impressive sight.

Medical matters that affect your visit.

Here are some medical matters that may affect your visit to Victoria Falls in Zimbabwe:

Vaccinations

It is recommended to be up to date on routine vaccines such as measles-mumps-rubella (MMR), diphtheria-tetanus-pertussis, and influenza. Additionally, vaccines for hepatitis A, typhoid, and yellow fever may be recommended depending on your travel history, activities planned, and current health status.

Malaria

Victoria Falls is located in a malaria-endemic region, so taking malaria prophylaxis medication is recommended. It is also important to use insect repellent, wear long sleeves and pants, and sleep under a mosquito net to prevent mosquito bites.

Traveler's diarrhea

Changes in diet and water quality can lead to traveler's diarrhea. It is important to drink bottled water, avoid consuming untreated water or raw/undercooked foods, and practice good hand hygiene.

Sun exposure

The sun in Zimbabwe can be intense, increasing the risk of sunburn and heat-related illnesses. It is important to use sunscreen with a high SPF, wear protective clothing, and stay hydrated while exploring the outdoors.

Altitude sickness

Victoria Falls is situated at a higher altitude, which can lead to altitude sickness in some individuals. Symptoms may include headache, dizziness, nausea, and fatigue. It is important to acclimate slowly, stay hydrated, and avoid exerting yourself too quickly.

Wildlife encounters

While exploring the natural surroundings of Victoria Falls, there is a possibility of encountering wildlife such as baboons, elephants, and hippos. It is important to keep a safe distance from animals, follow local guidelines, and avoid feeding or approaching wildlife.

Healthcare facilities

Medical facilities in Victoria Falls may not be as advanced as those in developed countries. It is recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and to carry a basic first aid kit with essential medications.

Always consult with a healthcare provider or travel medicine specialist before your trip to Victoria Falls to discuss any specific health concerns and receive personalized medical advice.

Cultural etiquettes at Victoria Falls

When visiting Victoria Falls, it is important to be respectful of the local culture. The people of Zimbabwe are very welcoming and friendly, but there are a few things to keep in mind. First, dress conservatively, especially when visiting villages and markets.

It is also important to avoid public displays of affection, as this is considered disrespectful. Second, it is important to ask permission before taking photos of people, as this is considered to be a sign of respect.

Third, it is important to be respectful of the environment. The falls are a natural wonder, and it is important to leave them as you found them. Avoid littering, and be sure to stay on designated paths. Be sure to also respect the wildlife.

Do not feed the animals, and keep a safe distance from them. Finally, be sure to engage with the local people. Ask them about their culture and way of life, and be open-minded and respectful.

Language and communication at Victoria Falls

The main mode of communication at Victoria Falls is English. However, there are also a few other languages spoken in the area, such as Ndebele and Shona. Most locals are able to understand and speak a little bit of English, so you should be able to communicate with them easily.

It is always a good idea to learn a few basic phrases in the local language before you travel. This will show that you are interested in learning about the culture and that you are respectful of their language.

Safety at Victoria Falls

Victoria Falls is a generally safe destination, but it is still important to take some basic safety precautions. First, always be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings. Second, do not leave your valuables unattended, especially at the falls.

Third, be cautious when swimming or participating in adventure activities. Make sure you follow all safety instructions and use the provided safety equipment. Finally, be aware of the wildlife. Do not approach or feed the animals, and be sure to keep your distance.

Vital information on money matters

Money matters at Victoria Falls are fairly straightforward. The official currency of Zimbabwe is the Zimbabwean dollar, but US dollars are also widely accepted. Most hotels, restaurants, and shops will accept either currency.

If you are planning on using US dollars, make sure to bring small denominations, as larger bills may not be accepted. You can also withdraw cash from ATMs in the area, but be aware that there may be a fee. Most businesses in the area accept credit cards, but it is always a good idea to have some cash on hand as well.

Finally, keep in mind that tipping is customary in Zimbabwe. It is common to tip 10-15% for services, and it is always appreciated.

Fun things to do at Victoria Falls

Visit Victoria Falls National Park

This national park offers stunning views of the falls from various viewpoints along walking trails. You can witness the power and beauty of the falls up close, and also spot local wildlife and lush vegetation in the park.

Take a Helicopter or Microlight Flight

Experience a unique and exhilarating perspective of Victoria Falls by taking a helicopter or microlight flight over the area. You'll get a bird's eye view of the falls and the surrounding landscape, providing a memorable and breathtaking experience.

White Water Rafting on the Zambezi River

For adrenaline seekers, white water rafting on the Zambezi River below the falls is a must-do activity. The river offers challenging rapids and breathtaking scenery, providing an unforgettable adventure for thrill-seekers.

Bungee Jumping

Victoria Falls is famous for its bungee jumping opportunities, particularly from the Victoria Falls Bridge. Take the plunge and experience the thrill of free-falling towards the Zambezi River with the iconic falls as your backdrop.

Swim in Devil's Pool

When water levels are low (usually during the dry season), visitors can swim in the famous Devil's Pool, a natural infinity pool right on the edge of the falls. It offers a unique and thrilling swimming experience with unbeatable views.

Sunset Cruises on the Zambezi River

Unwind and relax on a sunset cruise along the Zambezi River, taking in the beautiful scenery, enjoying a drink, and possibly spotting wildlife along the riverbanks. It's a perfect way to end a day of exploring Victoria Falls.

Canoe Safaris

Explore the Zambezi River on a guided canoe safari, where you can paddle past hippos, crocodiles, and various bird species while enjoying the tranquility of the river. It's a unique way to experience the natural beauty and wildlife of the region.

Cultural Tours

Learn about the local culture and history by taking a cultural tour in the nearby communities. Visit local villages, interact with residents, and gain insight into the traditions, customs, and way of life in the area.

These activities offer a diverse range of experiences for visitors to Victoria Falls, catering to adventure seekers, nature lovers, and those looking to immerse themselves in the beauty and culture of the region.

Who can travel to Victoria Falls?

Anyone can travel to Victoria Falls in Zimbabwe, regardless of their nationality. However, all visitors must have a valid passport and a visa. Citizens of certain countries may be able to obtain a visa upon arrival, while others must apply for a visa in advance.

It is important to check with the Zimbabwean embassy or consulate for specific requirements for your country. Once you have the proper documentation, you are free to explore the natural wonder of Victoria Falls

Travel Documents

You will need a valid passport and visa to enter Zimbabwe. Make sure your passport is valid for at least six months after your travel date and check if you need to obtain a visa before traveling.

What time of the year is best to visit?

Victoria Falls is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on what you want to see and do. The peak season for tourism is from June to October, when the weather is dry and the falls are at their most powerful. This is also the best time for wildlife viewing and adventure activities like white water rafting and bungee jumping.

If you want to avoid the crowds, the shoulder seasons of April to May and November to December are good options. The rainy season from December to March is considered the low season, but it can be a great time to visit if you're interested in bird watching or photography.

Packing essentials for your trip

Here is a list of packing essentials for a trip to Victoria Falls in Zimbabwe:

- Lightweight and breathable clothing for hot weather.

- Long-sleeved shirts and pants for protection against mosquitoes and the sun.

- Comfortable walking shoes or hiking boots.

- Swimwear for activities like swimming or rafting.

- Rain jacket or poncho for unexpected rain showers.

- Passport and travel documents.

- Sunscreen, sunglasses, and a sun hat.

- Insect repellent to protect against mosquitoes.

- Personal medications and a basic first aid kit.

- Camera or smartphone for capturing memories.

- Travel adaptor for charging your electronic devices.

- Power bank to keep your devices charged on the go.

- Travel-size toiletries and a reusable water bottle.

- Binoculars for wildlife viewing.

- A small backpack or daypack for carrying essentials during excursions.

- Map or GPS device to navigate around Victoria Falls.

- Emergency contact information and travel insurance details.

- Guidebooks or travel guides for information on the area.

- Lightweight and quick-drying towel.

- Snacks and drinks for day trips and excursions.

- Your favorite book or entertainment for downtime.

It's important to pack light but effectively for your trip to Victoria Falls, considering the weather conditions, planned activities, and duration of your stay.

Make sure to check the local weather forecast before packing to ensure you have the right clothing and gear for your trip.
