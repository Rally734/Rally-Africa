Views OF Fish River Canyon

About

location:

Ai Ais National Park, Namibia

The fish river is located in the southern part of Namibia, near the border with SouthAfrica. It is one of the most remarkable natural landmarks in the country, characterized by its breathtaking size and beauty. The canyon stretches approximately 160 kilometers (100 miles) in length, reaches widths of 27 kilometers(17 miles), and plunges to depths of around 550 meters (1,800 feet). It is the largest canyon in Africa and one of the largest in the world.

The fish River Canyon is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and adventurers alike, attracting hikers from around the world. The canyon is home to the Fish River Canyon Hiking Trail, a challenging 90-kilometer (56-mile) trek that takes about 5 to 6 days to complete. This trail is considered one of the best hiking experiences in Namibia and offers incredible views of the canyon, as well as opportunities to spot wildlife such as mountain Zebra and Kudu.

Things to know before travelling to Fish River Canyon

This article is a tip of the iceberg but will equip you with the "Absolute Need to Knows" for Fish River Canyon.

How to get there?

The Fish River Canyon is located in southern Namibia, about 350 kilometers (220 miles) from the capital city of Windhoek. The most common way to get there is by car, via the B4 and C13 highways. The drive takes about 4 to 5 hours, depending on the route you take.

Another option is to fly into Windhoek and then take a connecting flight to the town of Aussenkehr, which is the closest airport to the canyon. From Aussenkehr, you can arrange for a shuttle or car rental to the canyon.

About the weather.

The weather in the Fish River Canyon is generally hot and dry. The region lies in the southern part of Namibia, which is a desert area with very little rainfall. Temperatures can be extreme, with daytime highs reaching over 40 degrees Celsius(104 degrees Fahrenheit) in the summer months of November to February.

The nights are much cooler with temperatures dropping below freezing in the winter months of June to August. Winds can also be strong, especially in the afternoon. The Fish River Canyon is also known for its clear, starry skies, making it a great place for stargazing.

Medical matters that affect your visit.

Some medical matters that may affect your visit to Fish River Canyon include:

Heat-related illnesses

The canyon's hot and arid climate can lead to heat exhaustion or heat stroke if proper precautions are not taken. It is important to stay hydrated, wear appropriate clothing, and take regular breaks in the shade to prevent these conditions.

Dehydration

The dry climate and physical activity involved in hiking in the canyon can lead to dehydration if you do not drink enough fluids. Make sure to bring an adequate supply of water and drink regularly to stay hydrated.

Sunburn

The intense sun exposure in the canyon can lead to sunburn if you do not adequately protect your skin. It is important to wear sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat to avoid sun damage.

Wildlife encounters

While rare, encounters with wildlife such as snakes or scorpions in the canyon can pose a risk of envenomation or injury. Be aware of your surroundings and follow safety guidelines to minimize the risk of encounters with wildlife.

Pre-existing medical conditions

If you have pre-existing medical conditions such as heart disease, respiratory issues, or other chronic illnesses, the physical demands of hiking in the canyon may pose a risk to your health. It is advisable to consult with your healthcare provider before embarking on any strenuous activities in the canyon.

Altitude sickness

Although Fish River Canyon is not at a high altitude, some visitors may still experience symptoms of altitude sickness due to the changes in elevation. Symptoms may include headache, nausea, and fatigue. It is important to acclimate slowly and listen to your body to prevent altitude sickness.

Foot injuries

Hiking in the rugged terrain of Fish River Canyon can increase the risk of foot injuries such as blisters, sprains, or cuts. Wearing proper footwear and taking care of your feet can help prevent these injuries during your visit.

Remember to take necessary precautions, be prepared for emergencies, and seek medical attention if needed to ensure a safe and enjoyable visit to Fish River Canyon.

About the locals

The locals at Fish River Canyon are the Nama people, who are a subgroup of the Khoisan people. They are a nomadic people who have lived in the area for centuries, and they have a deep connection to the land.

The Nama people are known for their hospitality and their knowledge of the area, and they are often happy to share their culture and traditions with visitors. They are also skilled at making beautiful crafts, which can be found in the gift shops near the canyon.

One of the most interesting things about the Nama people is their traditional dress. Men typically wear long skirts and headbands, while women wear long dresses with intricate beadwork. The Nama people are also known for their unique musical instruments, such as the bow and calabash, which is made from a gourd and is used to make a distinctive clicking sound.

The Nama people are also skilled storytellers, and they have a rich oral history that they are happy to share with visitors.

The canyon is in a remote place with limited services

The Fish River Canyon is indeed a remote area, and there are no large towns or cities nearby. The closest town is Aussenkehr, which is about 50 kilometers (30 miles) away. While Aussenkehr does have a small grocery store, there are limited options for restaurants, hotels and other services.

The canyon itself is also fairly undeveloped, with just a few basic campsites and rest areas. Therefore, visitors should come prepared with all the supplies they need including food, water and any other necessities.

Cultural etiquettes at Fish River Canyon

There are a few things to keep in mind when it comes to cultural etiquette at Fish River Canyon. First, it is important to dress respectfully, as the Nama people are a conservative group. It is also important to avoid pointing at people or taking photos without asking permission first.

When meeting someone for the first time, it is customary to greet them with a handshake. If you are a woman, it is also polite to greet someone by lowering your head and saying "Awa", which means "hello".

Second, when entering someone's home, it is customary to remove your shoes before entering. It is also important to avoid touching anyone's head, as it is considered disrespectful.

Additionally, it is considered rude to ask questions about someone's age or marital status. Lastly, it is important to respect the local wildlife and refrain from disturbing the environment.

Language and communication at Fish River Canyon

The primary mode of communication at Fish River Canyon is Afrikaans, which is spoken by the Nama people. However, most of the locals also speak some English, so you should be able to communicate with them if you speak either language.

In addition to verbal communication, the Nama people also communicate through nonverbal gestures and body language. For example, they may use hand gestures to indicate that they are happy, sad, or angry. They may also use touch to show affection or respect.

Safety at Fish River Canyon

Safety is a top priority at Fish River Canyon, and there are a few things to keep in mind to stay safe. First, it is important to stay hydrated and to bring plenty of water with you when exploring the canyon. Second, it is important to wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.

Third, it is important to stay on the designated trails and to not attempt to climb any cliffs or jump into the canyon. Finally, it is important to be aware of your surroundings and to stay alert for snakes and other wildlife.

Vital information on money matters

Fish River Canyon is located in Namibia, and the official currency is the Namibian dollar. However, South African rand is also widely accepted. You can exchange money at banks, hotels, and other places of business.

Most major credit cards are also accepted. As for tipping, it is customary to tip 10-15% at restaurants and for other services. Keep in mind that some businesses may add a 10% service charge to your bill, so be sure to check before you tip.

One of the most important things to know about money matters at Fish River Canyon is that bargaining is part of the culture. Many businesses expect you to haggle over prices, so don't be afraid to negotiate.

It is also important to be aware of the current exchange rate and to keep an eye on your spending, as the cost of living can be higher than you might expect. Lastly, it is always a good idea to bring a little extra cash in case of emergencies.

Fun things to do at the Fish River Canyon

Hiking

One of the most popular activities at Fish River Canyon is hiking. There are various trails of different lengths and difficulties that allow you to explore the canyon and enjoy breathtaking views. The multi-day hike along the Fish River Canyon is a challenging but rewarding adventure for experienced hikers.

Scenic Drives

You can take a scenic drive along the rim of the canyon to enjoy panoramic views of the vast landscape. There are designated viewpoints that offer spectacular photo opportunities, especially during sunrise or sunset.

Photography

Fish River Canyon provides ample opportunities for photography enthusiasts to capture stunning landscapes, rock formations, and the play of light and shadows in this natural wonder. The changing colors and textures of the canyon make for fantastic photographic subjects.

Stargazing

The remote location of Fish River Canyon makes it an ideal spot for stargazing. With minimal light pollution, you can witness a dazzling display of stars, planets, and constellations overhead. Bring a blanket and lay under the vast night sky for a truly magical experience.

Birdwatching

The canyon is home to a variety of bird species, making it a paradise for birdwatchers. Keep an eye out for eagles, vultures, and other raptors soaring above the canyon, as well as other unique bird species in the area.

Camping

Set up camp near the canyon rim and spend a night under the stars. Camping at Fish River Canyon allows you to immerse yourself in nature and experience the peaceful surroundings away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Sunset/Sunrise Viewing

Witnessing the sunrise or sunset at Fish River Canyon is a magical experience. The changing colors of the sky reflecting off the canyon walls create a mesmerizing visual spectacle that is truly unforgettable.

Nature Walks

Take leisurely walks along the canyon rim to observe the flora and fauna of the surrounding area. You may encounter interesting plant species adapted to the harsh desert environment and possibly spot wildlife such as lizards, antelope, or other desert dwellers.

These activities offer a variety of ways to enjoy and appreciate the natural beauty of Fish River Canyon while creating memorable experiences in this stunning landscape.

Who can travel to Fish River Canyon?

The Fish River Canyon is open to all types of travelers, from solo adventurers to families and groups. That being said, there are some important to keep in mind when planning a trip to the canyon. The main factor is the physical demands of visiting the canyon. The area is very remote and there are some long hikes involved.

As such, it's best suited for travelers who are in good physical shape and comfortable with spending time outdoors. In addition, it's important to remember that the area is very hot and dry, so travelers should be prepared for the heat and the lack of amenities.

Travel Documents

You will need a valid passport and visa to enter Namibia. Make sure your passport is valid for at least six months after your travel date and check if you need to obtain a visa before traveling.

What time of the year is best to visit?

If you're looking to visit the Fish River Canyon, the best time to go is during the cooler, dry season from April to September. During this time, the days are milder and less humid, making for more comfortable conditions when hiking and exploring the canyon.

In addition, the rainfall is low, making it easier to navigate the landscape. The dry season is also when the wildlife is more active, so it's a great time for animal spotting. Finally, the nights are much cooler, so you'll be able to enjoy the spectacular starry skies in the evenings.
