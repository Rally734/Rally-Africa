Views Of African Renaissance Monument

About

location:

Dakar, Senegal

The African Renaissance Monument is a massive bronze statue located in Dakar, Senegal. The statue was designed by the Senegalese sculptor, Pierre Goudiaby, and it was inaugurated in 2010.

The statue is a symbol of the African Renaissance, and it's meant to represent the hopes and aspirations of the African people. The statue is 49 meters tall, making it one of the tallest statues in Africa. It depicts a man, a woman, and a child who are reaching up towards the sky.

In addition to being an impressive feat of engineering and artistry, the African Renaissance Monument is also a symbol of Senegal's history and culture. The statue is located in a place called "Pointe des Almadies," which is the westernmost point of the African continent.

This location was chosen to symbolize Africa's connection to the rest of the world. In addition, the monument is surrounded by a park that features a variety of sculptures and monuments that represent African cultures and traditions. It's truly a unique and thought-provoking place to visit.
