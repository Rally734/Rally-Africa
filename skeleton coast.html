Views Of Skeleton Coast

About

location:

Northern part of the Atlantic Coast, Namibia

The Skeleton Coast is a stretch of Namibian coastline known for its treacherous conditions, foggy weather, strong currents, and numerous shipwrecks scattered along the shore.

The name "Skeleton Coast" originates from the whale and seal bones that used to litter the beaches from the whaling industry, as well as the skeletons of ships and sailors that met their demise in the harsh environment.

This barren and desolate region is characterized by its stark beauty, with vast sand dunes, rocky outcrops, and the cold Benguela Current of the Atlantic Ocean pounding the shore. The area is home to a diverse array of wildlife, including seals, dolphins, and seabirds, adapted to survive in this harsh coastal environment.

Visitors to the Skeleton Coast can explore its rugged landscapes, take in the eerie shipwrecks that dot the coastline, and witness the unique flora and fauna that thrives in this harsh environment.

The Skeleton Coast is also known for its remote and untouched beauty, making it a popular destination for adventurers, photographers, and nature lovers looking to experience a truly wild and untamed part of Namibia.

Things to know before travelling to Skeleton Coast

This article is a tip of the iceberg but will equip you with the "Absolute Need to Knows" for Skeleton Coast.

How to get there?

To visit Skeleton Coast, you can fly into the Hosea Kutako International Airport in Windhoek, the capital of Namibia. From there, you can take a domestic flight to Walvis Bay. From Walvis Bay, you can rent a car and drive to Skeleton Coast.

The drive will take you through the desert and provide you with stunning views of the landscape. Make sure to have a reliable GPS or map as the region is remote and can be challenging to navigate.

There are also regular bus services that run from Walvis Bay to the Skeleton Coast National Park. The main bus company that operates these routes is Intercape, and their buses depart from the Walvis Bay station in the city center.

The journey takes about 3 hours, and you'll need to make sure to book in advance, as the buses can fill up quickly, especially during peak season. The bus is a great option if you're on a budget, and it's also a fun way to see the Namibian countryside!

Medical matters that affect your visit.

The following are some common medical concerns for visitors to Skeleton Coast:

Sunburn

The Skeleton Coast in Namibia is known for its intense sun exposure due to the desert environment and proximity to the equator. It is important to protect your skin from sunburn by applying sunscreen, wearing protective clothing, and seeking shade during the hottest parts of the day.

Dehydration

The desert climate of the Skeleton Coast can lead to rapid dehydration, especially if you are engaging in physical activities like hiking or exploring the area. It is crucial to stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water throughout your visit.

Heat Exhaustion

Extreme temperatures in the Skeleton Coast can increase the risk of heat-related illnesses such as heat exhaustion. Symptoms include dizziness, fatigue, nausea, and headache. To prevent heat exhaustion, stay in shaded areas, drink water frequently, and avoid strenuous activities during the hottest times of the day.

Sandstorms

Sandstorms are common in desert environments like the Skeleton Coast and can lead to respiratory issues for some individuals. It is important to cover your mouth and nose with a cloth or mask during a sandstorm to prevent inhaling sand particles.

Snakes and insect bites

The Skeleton Coast is home to various wildlife, including snakes and insects. Some species may be venomous and their bites can lead to medical emergencies. Be cautious when exploring the area and wear appropriate footwear and clothing to protect yourself from bites.

Wilderness injuries

While exploring the rugged terrain of the Skeleton Coast, there is a risk of injuries such as cuts, bruises, sprains, or fractures. It is advisable to wear appropriate footwear, carry a first aid kit, and be cautious when walking on uneven surfaces to minimize the risk of accidents.

Medical facilities

The Skeleton Coast is a remote and isolated region with limited access to medical facilities. In the event of a medical emergency, it may be challenging to receive immediate medical attention. It is recommended to be prepared with necessary medications, first aid supplies, and emergency contact information before visiting the area.

It is important to consult with a healthcare provider before traveling to the Skeleton Coast to ensure you are prepared for any potential medical matters that may arise during your visit.

The Skeleton coast is located in a remote area

The Skeleton Coast is a remote and desolate stretch of coastline along the northwestern coast of Namibia and southwestern Angola. It is known for its harsh and unforgiving conditions, with strong ocean currents, thick fog, and treacherous sand dunes.

The area got its name from the numerous shipwrecks that dot the coastline, giving it an eerie and haunting atmosphere.

Due to its remote location and extreme conditions, the Skeleton Coast is largely inaccessible and uninhabited, making it a unique and challenging destination for adventurous travelers. The landscape is characterized by stark desert plains, towering sand dunes, and rugged cliffs that drop off into the cold waters of the Atlantic Ocean.

The Skeleton Coast is home to a variety of wildlife, including seals, dolphins, and seabirds, as well as some adapted desert-dwelling species. The area is also known for its spectacular sunsets and dramatic coastal scenery, making it a popular destination for photographers and nature enthusiasts.

Overall, the Skeleton Coast's remote and rugged beauty make it a truly special and otherworldly destination for those willing to venture off the beaten path.

Park's permit and regulation

There are a few permits and regulations that visitors to the Skeleton Coast need to be aware of. First, a permit is required to enter the Skeleton Coast National Park, and it can be purchased at the park's gate or online. Additionally, there are speed limits and driving regulations that must be followed while in the park.

Driving off-road and off-trail is not allowed, and all visitors must stay on designated roads and trails. And finally, there are strict regulations around the collection of marine resources, such as shells, coral, and sea life, so visitors should be aware of these regulations before visiting.

Safety at Skeleton Coast

Safety is an important consideration when visiting the Skeleton Coast, as it is a very remote and isolated area. It's important to be prepared for any potential emergencies, and to always follow the advice of park rangers and staff.

One of the biggest safety concerns in the area is the extreme weather conditions. The Skeleton Coast is known for its fierce winds, sandstorms, and cold temperatures. It's also important to be aware of the potential for flash floods, as the area is prone to sudden rainfall.

Wildlife and Natural hazard at the Skeleton Coast

The Skeleton Coast is home to a variety of wildlife, but it's also a place where the forces of nature are particularly intense. The area is known for its sandstorms, which can happen suddenly and be extremely dangerous. Sandstorms can quickly reduce visibility, and the winds can be strong enough to blow over vehicles.

Flash floods are also a common hazard in the area, and they can be especially dangerous when combined with the strong winds. In addition to the natural hazards, it's important to be aware of the wildlife. Some of the animals, like lions and elephants, can be dangerous if you approach them or surprise them.

About the locals

The locals who live near the Skeleton Coast are the Himba people, who have a unique culture and way of life. The Himba are known for their distinctive red ochre body paint, which they use to protect their skin from the harsh sun and to keep insects away. They are also known for their intricate jewelry and traditional dress.

The Himba are semi-nomadic, and they often move around to find fresh water and grazing land for their cattle. They also have a strong connection to the land and their ancestors, and they believe that they must respect the spirits of the land in order to maintain balance

They are often photographed by tourists, but it's important to ask permission before taking photos, and to respect their privacy. It's also important to remember that the Skeleton Coast is a sacred place for the Himba, and it's not appropriate to take any natural objects from the area.

Language and communication at Skeleton Coast

The Skeleton Coast, in Namibia, is home to a diverse array of languages and dialects. The predominant language spoken on the coast is Oshiwambo, but other languages such as Damara, Herero, and Nama are also spoken.

English is also widely spoken, and is the official language of Namibia. Because of the region's history of contact with a wide variety of cultures, there is also a significant degree of linguistic variation and complexity.

Many people are multilingual, and it is not uncommon for people to switch between languages in the course of a single conversation. Additionally, there is a strong influence of nonverbal communication, as hand gestures, facial expressions, and body language play an important role in daily life.

The diversity of language and communication on the Skeleton Coast reflects the region's complex history and its diverse cultural heritage. The ability to speak multiple languages and to communicate in a variety of ways is a vital part of life on the coast.

Vital information on money matters

When it comes to money matters at the Skeleton Coast, it's important to know that the Namibian dollar is the local currency. The Namibian dollar is pegged to the South African rand, and it can be used interchangeably with the rand throughout Namibia.

Credit cards are accepted in most places, but it's a good idea to have some cash on hand as well. The nearest ATMs are in the town of Swakopmund, so it's a good idea to withdraw money before heading to the Skeleton Coast. It's also a good idea to bring small bills, as change is often difficult to get.

Another important thing to know about money matters at the Skeleton Coast is that many of the local people are not used to bargaining. When shopping at local markets, it's best to pay the asking price, rather than trying to haggle.

This is out of respect for the local culture and traditions. It's also a good idea to ask before taking any photos of the vendors or their wares. If you're interested in buying any traditional crafts, it's a good idea to support the local artisans by buying directly from them, rather than through a middleman.

Fun things to do at The Skeleton Coast

The Skeleton Coast in Namibia offers a unique and diverse range of activities for visitors to enjoy. Here are some fun things to do at the Skeleton Coast:

Scenic Flights

Experience the breathtaking views of the Skeleton Coast from the air by taking a scenic flight. This allows you to see the rugged coastline, shipwrecks, and diverse wildlife from a different perspective.

Visit shipwrecks

Explore the eerie and fascinating shipwrecks that dot the coastline of the Skeleton Coast. These remnants of past maritime disasters provide a glimpse into the area's history and add to its mysterious allure.

Sandboarding

Try your hand at sandboarding on the towering sand dunes near the Skeleton Coast. Whether you are a beginner or an experienced sandboarder, the dunes offer a thrilling and adventurous experience for all.

Wildlife viewing

The Skeleton Coast is home to a variety of wildlife, including seals, dolphins, whales, and numerous bird species. Take a guided wildlife tour to see these animals up close in their natural habitat.

Guided Tours

Join a guided tour of the Skeleton Coast to learn about the area's history, geology, and wildlife from knowledgeable local guides. They can provide insights into the region's unique ecosystem and help you appreciate its beauty even more.

Explore the Desert Adapted Wildlife

The Skeleton Coast is home to a variety of desert-adapted wildlife, including desert elephants, lions, and brown hyenas. Take a guided safari to spot these unique animals in their natural habitat.

Photography

The Skeleton Coast offers incredible opportunities for photography, with its stark landscapes, dramatic seascapes, and abundant wildlife. Capture stunning images of the coastline, shipwrecks, wildlife, and dunes to create lasting memories of your trip.

Fishing

The Skeleton Coast is a great destination for fishing enthusiasts, with opportunities to catch a variety of fish species in the Atlantic Ocean. Whether you prefer shore fishing or deep-sea fishing, the area offers a unique fishing experience.

Hiking

Explore the rugged terrain of the Skeleton Coast on foot by taking a guided hiking tour. Discover hidden gems, interesting flora and fauna, and stunning viewpoints as you traverse this unique landscape.

These are just a few of the fun activities you can enjoy at the Skeleton Coast in Namibia. Remember to respect the environment and wildlife, and follow any regulations or guidelines in place to preserve this special place for future generations to enjoy.

Who can travel to The Skeleton Island?

Anyone can travel to the Skeleton Coast National Park, but it's important to be aware that the park is quite remote and it can be a challenging destination. The area is known for its rough terrain, unpredictable weather, and lack of infrastructure.

It's best suited for people who are looking for an adventure and who are comfortable with the idea of being off the beaten path. The park is not suitable for people with limited mobility or young children.

Travel Documents

You will need a valid passport and visa to enter Namibia. Make sure your passport is valid for at least six months after your travel date and check if you need to obtain a visa before traveling.

What time of the year is best to visit?

The best time to visit the Skeleton Coast National Park is during the dry season, from May to September. During this time, the weather is milder and more predictable, making it easier to navigate the park.

The temperatures are also cooler during this time, making it more comfortable for visitors. It's important to keep in mind that the park is very popular during this time of year, so it's best to book your trip well in advance.
