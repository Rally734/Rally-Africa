Views Of The Great Migration

About

location:

Serengeti national park, Tanzania

The Great Migration in Tanzania is an annual event in which millions of Wildebeests and Zebras migrate from the Serengeti National Park in Tanzania to the Masai Mara National Reserve in Kenya. The migration typically starts in May, when the animals head north in search of fresh grazing lands. In July, the animals reach the Grumeti River, where they must cross the crocodile-infested waters to continue their journey.

As the animals continue their journey north, they eventually reach the Mara River, which they must also cross. This river crossing is considered the most dangerous part of the migration, as the animals must swim through strong currents and dodge crocodiles in order to make it to the other side.

In October, the animals reach the Masai Mara, where they spend the dry season grazing on the grasslands. In November, the animals begin their return journey south to the Serengeti. Overall, the Great Migration is an incredible spectacle of nature that is truly a sight to behold.

Things to know before travelling to The Great Migration

This article is a tip of the iceberg but will equip you with the "Absolute Need to Knows" for The Great Migration.

How to get there?

The best way to get into the Great Migration is by flying into the Kilimanjaro International Airport,which is located near Arusha. From there, it's a 4-5 hour drive to the Serengeti National Park. Alternatively, you can fly directly to the Serengeti via a light aircraft. There are several airstrips in the Serengeti that are used for the safari flights.

Once you're in the Serengeti, you'll need to take a safari vehicle to get to the migration. It's best to book a safari tour in advance, as the roads can be difficult to navigate without a guide.

About the weather

The weather in the Serengeti during the Great Migration is typically dry and warm, with daytime temperatures ranging from 25-30 degrees Celsius (77-86 degrees Fahrenheit). It's important to pack clothing that is lightweight and breathable. The nights can be quite cool, so it's a good idea to bring a light jacket or sweater for the evenings.

In addition, the rain can sometimes disrupt travel plans, so it's important to be flexible and prepared for changes in the weather.

Medical matters that affect your visit.

Medical matters that may affect your visit to the Great Migration in Tanzania include:

Vaccinations

Before traveling to Tanzania for the Great Migration, it is essential to be up-to-date on routine vaccines such as measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis (DTaP), varicella (chickenpox), polio, and influenza.

Additionally, certain travel vaccines like yellow fever, hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and meningitis may be recommended based on your individual health status and travel itinerary.

Malaria

Tanzania is a malaria-endemic country, and travelers to the region should take precautions to prevent mosquito bites and consider taking antimalarial medication as recommended by a healthcare provider.

Traveler's diarrhea

Changes in diet and exposure to unfamiliar bacteria can increase the risk of traveler's diarrhea. It is essential to practice good hygiene, such as washing hands regularly, drinking bottled or boiled water, and avoiding consuming raw or undercooked foods.

Sun exposure

The sun in Tanzania can be intense, so it is crucial to protect yourself from sunburn by using sunscreen, wearing protective clothing, and seeking shade during peak hours.

Altitude sickness

Parts of Tanzania, such as the Ngorongoro Crater, are at high altitudes, which can lead to altitude sickness in some individuals. It is important to acclimatize slowly, stay hydrated, and consider medication if necessary.

Wildlife encounters

The Great Migration offers a unique opportunity to see wildlife up close, but it also comes with some risks. It is essential to follow park regulations, maintain a safe distance from animals, and be aware of the potential for insect bites or stings.

Medical facilities

While Tanzania has some good medical facilities in major cities, remote areas may have limited access to healthcare services. Travelers should consider purchasing travel insurance that includes medical evacuation coverage in case of emergencies.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare provider or travel medicine specialist well in advance of your trip to the Great Migration in Tanzania to discuss any specific medical concerns, receive personalized recommendations, and ensure a safe and healthy journey.

Cultural etiquettes at The Great Migration

The Great Migration is a culturally significant event in Tanzania, so it is important to be respectful of the local culture. When visiting the Serengeti National Park, dress modestly and avoid wearing bright colors. It is also important to avoid taking photos of the Maasai people without their permission.

When interacting with the Maasai, always greet them with a smile and a handshake. Do not make any sudden movements or loud noises, and try to remain calm and respectful. Remember that the Maasai are proud of their culture and they may be curious about your culture as well.

Language and Communication at The Great Migration

The Great Migration is an event that brings people from all over the world to witness the spectacle of over 2 million wildebeest and zebra crossing the plains of the Serengeti. As such, you will find that many languages are spoken at the Great Migration.

English is the most common language, but you will also hear Swahili, German, French, and Italian. The main language spoken by the local Maasai people is Maa.

Even if you do not speak any of these languages, you should not have any trouble communicating with the locals, as most people are very friendly and welcoming.

Safety at The Great Migration

The Great Migration is a generally safe event to attend, but there are some safety concerns to be aware of. It is important to stay with your group and to follow the instructions of your guide at all times. Do not wander off on your own, as the area is home to dangerous wildlife.

Additionally, you should be wary of pickpockets and scams. Be sure to keep your belongings secure and do not accept gifts from strangers. Finally, always listen to the local authorities and follow their instructions in case of an emergency.

Vital information on money matters

Money matters are an important consideration for anyone visiting the Great Migration. The currency in Tanzania is the Tanzanian shilling, and most places will accept both cash and credit cards. However, it is always a good idea to have some local currency on hand, as many of the smaller businesses may not accept credit cards.

You can exchange money at banks or at bureaux de change, but be sure to check the exchange rate beforehand. It is also a good idea to have some smaller bills for tips and to bargain with vendors.

Fun things to do at The Great Migration

Here are some fun things to do at the Great Migration in Tanzania:

Witness the Migration

The Great Migration is one of the most spectacular natural events on the planet, where millions of wildebeest, zebras, and other animals move across the Serengeti in search of food and water. Watching this incredible sight is a must-do activity.

Game Drives

Take a safari game drive to get up close to the migrating herds and the predators that follow them. You'll have the opportunity to see lions, cheetahs, leopards, and other wildlife in action.

Balloon Safaris

Experience the Great Migration from a unique perspective by taking a hot air balloon safari over the Serengeti. Enjoy breathtaking views of the savannah and wildlife below.

Cultural Experiences

Visit local Maasai villages to learn about their culture, traditions, and way of life. You can participate in traditional dances, crafts, and even sample local cuisine.

Photography

The Great Migration offers incredible opportunities for photographers to capture stunning wildlife shots. Don't forget your camera and make sure to have enough memory cards!

Walking Safaris

Explore the Serengeti on foot with a guided walking safari, allowing you to get closer to nature and experience the sights and sounds of the bush up close.

Hot Springs Visit

After a long day of safari adventures, relax and rejuvenate at the nearby hot springs in Lake Manyara National Park. Enjoy a dip in the warm mineral waters surrounded by beautiful landscapes.

Bird Watching

The Serengeti is home to a wide variety of bird species, making it a paradise for birdwatchers. Keep an eye out for colorful birds like lilac-breasted rollers, ostriches, and secretary birds.

Picnic Lunches

Enjoy a bush lunch in the heart of the Serengeti, surrounded by incredible wildlife and landscapes. Many safari operators offer picnic lunches as part of their packages.

Sunset Cocktails

Wind down your day of adventure with a sundowner cocktail overlooking the Serengeti plains. Watch the sun dip below the horizon as you reflect on the day's experiences.

Remember to plan your trip well in advance, as the Great Migration is a popular event and accommodations can book up quickly.

Enjoy your time in Tanzania and make the most of this incredible natural phenomenon!

Who can travel to the Great Migration?

Anyone who is interested in wildlife and nature can travel to the Serengeti to witness the Great Migration. However, it's important to keep in mind that it's a challenging destination, with long drives and rugged roads. Therefore, it's best suited for those who are physically fit and up for an adventure.

Additionally, travelers should be aware that there is limited medical care in the area, so it's important to be prepared for any medical emergencies. It's also a good idea to use a reputable tour operator who can guide you safely through the region.

Travel Documents

You will need a valid passport and visa to enter Tanzania. Make sure your passport is valid for at least six months after your travel date and check if you need to obtain a visa before traveling.

What time of the year is best to visit?

The best time to visit the Serengeti to witness the Great Migration is from June to October, during the dry season. During this time,the wildebeests and Zebras are on the move, and it's an amazing sight to see. In addition, the roads are usually in better condition during the dry season, making it easier to travel to the migration.

Another great time to visit is from December to Febuary, when the Wildebeests calving season occurs. It's an incredible experience to see the newborn calves running with their mothers through the grasslands.

Packing essentials for ypur trip

Here is a list of packing essentials for your trip to the Great Migration in Tanzania:

Lightweight Clothing

Pack comfortable, lightweight clothing suitable for warm weather. Opt for breathable fabrics like cotton or moisture-wicking materials to stay cool during the day.

Comfortable Footwear

Bring sturdy walking shoes or hiking boots for exploring the terrain. Sandals or flip-flops may also come in handy for relaxing around the campsite.

Hat and Sunglasses

Protect yourself from the sun with a wide-brimmed hat and UV-blocking sunglasses.

Sunscreen and Insect Repellent

Tanzania can get very sunny and mosquitoes can be prevalent, so pack sunscreen with a high SPF and insect repellent to keep bugs at bay.

Binoculars and Camera

Don't forget to bring binoculars for wildlife viewing and a camera with extra batteries or memory cards to capture the memorable moments of the Great Migration.

Portable Charger

Keep your electronic devices charged with a portable charger to ensure you don't miss any photo opportunities.

Reusable Water Bottle

Stay hydrated by bringing a reusable water bottle to refill throughout the day.

Medical Kit

Pack a basic medical kit with essential items like pain relievers, bandages, antiseptic wipes, and any personal medications you may need.

Travel Documents and ID

Make sure to carry your passport, travel insurance information, and any necessary visas or permits for your trip.

Cash and Credit Cards

Have a mix of cash (in local currency) and credit cards for any purchases or emergencies that may arise during your trip.

Remember to pack light and only bring what you need for your safari adventure in Tanzania. Enjoy the breathtaking sights and wildlife encounters during the Great Migration!
