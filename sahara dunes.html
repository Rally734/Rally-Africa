Views Of Sahara Dunes

About

location:

Merzouga, Morocco

The Sahara dunes in Morocco are a part of the vast Sahara Desert, which is the largest hot desert in the world, covering much of North Africa. The Sahara desert extends across several countries including Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia, Libya, Egypt, Mali, Mauritania, and Niger.

In Morocco, the Sahara desert is known for its stunning sand dunes, which are some of the most iconic and recognizable features of the desert landscape. The Erg Chebbi and Erg Chigaga dunes are two of the most popular dune formations in Morocco.

Erg Chebbi, located near the town of Merzouga in southeastern Morocco, is known for its towering orange sand dunes that can reach heights of up to 150 meters. Visitors to Erg Chebbi can experience thrilling activities such as camel treks, sandboarding, and camping under the stars in traditional Berber desert camps.

Erg Chigaga is another impressive dune area located in the region of Zagora, further south than Erg Chebbi. These dunes are less visited and provide a more remote and tranquil desert experience for those looking to escape the crowds.

Exploring the Sahara dunes in Morocco offers a unique and unforgettable experience, allowing visitors to witness the breathtaking beauty of the desert landscape and immerse themselves in the rich Berber culture and history of the region.

Things to know before travelling to The Sahara Dunes.

This article is a tip of the iceberg but will equip you with the "Absolute Need to Knows" for The Sahara Dunes.

How to get there?

If you want to visit the Sahara dunes in Morocco, there are a few different ways to get there. The most common way is to fly into Ouarzazate or Zagora, and then take a bus or taxi to the dunes. Another option is to take a tour from Marrakech or another nearby city.

There are also a few tour companies that offer desert safaris, which are a great way to see the dunes and the surrounding area. The Sahara dunes are a popular destination, so there are plenty of options for getting there.

About the weather

The weather in the Sahara dunes can be extreme and unpredictable. The daytime temperature in the summer can reach over 100 degrees Fahrenheit(38 degrees Celsius), while the night time temperature can drop below freezing. In the winter, the daytime temperature can be milder, but the nights can be quite cold.

The weather is also very dry, with very little rainfall throughout the year. It's important to be prepared for the extremes of temperature and to bring plenty of water and sun protection. In addition, the wind can be strong and gusty, and sandstorms are common.

Medical matters that affect your visit

Here are some medical matters that may affect your visit to the Sahara dunes:

Dehydration

The hot and arid climate of the Sahara desert can lead to rapid water loss through sweating and evaporation. It is important to stay well hydrated by drinking plenty of water before and during your visit to prevent dehydration.

Heatstroke

The extreme temperatures in the Sahara desert can increase the risk of heatstroke, a condition that occurs when the body overheats and is unable to regulate its temperature. It is important to stay cool, seek shade, and avoid strenuous activity during the hottest parts of the day.

Sunburn

The intense sun exposure in the desert can lead to sunburn if adequate sun protection measures are not taken. It is essential to wear sunscreen with a high SPF, protective clothing, and sunglasses to prevent sunburn.

Sandstorms

Windy conditions in the Sahara desert can cause sandstorms, which may lead to respiratory issues for some individuals. It is advisable to wear a face mask or scarf to protect your airways during sandstorms.

Insect bites

The desert environment is home to various insects, including mosquitoes and flies, which can transmit diseases such as malaria and dengue fever. It is essential to use insect repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing to prevent insect bites.

Allergies

Some individuals may be sensitive to desert plants, pollen, or dust, leading to allergic reactions such as hay fever or asthma. It is important to bring necessary medications and consult a healthcare provider before your visit.

Physical fitness

Trekking or exploring the Sahara dunes can be physically demanding, requiring stamina and endurance. Individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or limited physical fitness should consult a healthcare provider before embarking on such activities.

It is crucial to be prepared for these medical matters and take necessary precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable visit to the Sahara dunes.

A reputable tour with knowledgable locals is essential

Some travel bloggers would recommend renting a jeep and doing the Sahara on your own. But honestly, this is not a responsible recommendation. There is no signal in most areas of the Sahara. It even gets to a point where there are no roads and you're just driving over sand dunes.

Even someone from Marrakesh would have no idea where they are going out there. That's why we would definitely recommend visiting the Sahara with a tour group.

Cultural etiquettes at Sahara dunes

There are a few key cultural etiquettes to keep in mind when visiting the Sahara dunes in Morocco. First, it is important to dress modestly, covering your shoulders and knees. Second, it is important to show respect for local customs and traditions.

Third, be aware that bargaining is a part of the culture, so you may need to haggle when shopping in the souks. Finally, be respectful of the local people and try to learn some basic Arabic phrases to show your appreciation for the culture.

Language and communication at Sahara dunes

In terms of communication, most people in the Sahara dunes speak Arabic. However, French is also widely spoken, and many people also speak English. You may find that in smaller villages and towns, fewer people speak English, so it may be helpful to learn some basic Arabic phrases before you go.

If you are traveling with a tour group, your guide will likely be able to translate for you. Additionally, most hotels and restaurants in the area will have someone who speaks English.

Safety at Sahara dunes

Safety is an important consideration when visiting the Sahara dunes in Morocco. While the area is generally safe, it is always important to exercise caution when traveling. It is a good idea to travel with a guide or in a group, and to be aware of your surroundings at all times.

The sand can get extremely hot during the day, so it is important to wear appropriate shoes and clothing. There is also a risk of sandstorms, so it is important to stay up to date on the weather forecast.

Vital information on money matters

Money matters are an important consideration when traveling to the Sahara dunes in Morocco. The currency used in Morocco is the Moroccan dirham, and it is not possible to obtain it outside of the country. It is recommended to bring enough cash for your trip, as credit cards are not widely accepted.

It is also important to be aware of the exchange rate, as it can fluctuate. There are a few ways to get cash while in Morocco, including ATMs and currency exchange offices. Additionally, it is a good idea to bring small bills, as change can be hard to come by.

Fun things to do at Sahara Dunes

Desert Camping

One of the most popular activities at the Sahara dunes in Morocco is camping under the stars. You can experience the magic of the desert by setting up a campsite amidst the dunes and enjoying a night of stargazing.

Camel Trekking

Explore the vastness of the Sahara desert on a camel trek. You can ride through the dunes and experience the traditional way of traveling in the desert.

Sandboarding

For some adrenaline-pumping fun, try sandboarding on the dunes. Slide down the sandy slopes on a sandboard and enjoy the thrill of this unique desert sport.

Sunset/Sunrise Views

Witness the breathtaking views of the sunrise or sunset over the Sahara dunes. The changing colors of the sky and the play of light on the sand create a magical experience that you won't soon forget.

Berber Camp Visit

Immerse yourself in the local culture by visiting a Berber camp in the desert. Learn about the traditions and way of life of the Berber people, and enjoy a traditional Moroccan meal prepared in the desert.

Desert Hiking

Explore the desert on foot and discover the unique landscapes and flora and fauna of the Sahara. Guided hikes can take you to hidden oases, ancient ruins, and other points of interest in the desert.

Star Gazing

Experience the stunning night sky of the desert by stargazing in the Sahara. The lack of light pollution makes for excellent visibility, allowing you to see countless stars, planets, and even the Milky Way.

Photography

The Sahara dunes offer endless opportunities for photography, with its stunning landscapes, shifting light patterns, and unique desert flora and fauna. Capture the beauty of the desert on camera and create unforgettable memories.

Overall, the Sahara dunes in Morocco offer a wide range of fun and memorable activities for visitors to enjoy.

Whether you prefer adventure, relaxation, cultural experiences, or simply immersing yourself in the natural beauty of the desert, there is something for everyone to enjoy at this unique destination.

Who can travel to Sahara Dunes?

Anyone can travel to the Sahara dunes in Morocco, but there are some factors to consider. The terrain is rough and uneven, so the trip may not be suitable for those with mobility issues. Additionally, the climate is very hot and dry, so it is important to be prepared for the heat.

The Sahara dunes are also very remote, so it is important to have a reliable transportation plan. Finally, the facilities in the area are limited, so it is important to be prepared with the necessary supplies.

Travel Documents

You will need a valid passport and visa to enter Morocco. Make sure your passport is valid for at least six months after your travel date and check if you need to obtain a visa before traveling.

What time of the year is best to visit?

Generally speaking, the best time to visit the Sahara dunes in Morocco is during the cooler months of October to April. During these months, the temperatures are more moderate, making it easier to explore the dunes and enjoy the scenery.

Additionally, there is less dust and sandstorms during these months, making for a more pleasant experience. It is important to note that the weather can be unpredictable, so it is always a good idea to check the forecast before your trip.

Packing essentials for your trip

Packing Essentials for a Trip to Sahara Dunes:

Water and Hydration Supplies

Given the extreme heat and dry conditions in the Sahara Desert, it is crucial to stay hydrated. Bring a sufficient supply of water and consider carrying a hydration pack or water bottles.

Lightweight Clothing

Pack light-colored, loose-fitting clothing made from breathable fabrics like cotton or linen to help you stay cool and protect your skin from the sun. Long sleeves and pants can also provide protection against sunburn and sand.

Sun Protection

Bring a wide-brimmed hat, sunglasses with UV protection, and a high SPF sunscreen to shield yourself from the intense sunlight and prevent sunburn.

Headgear and Scarves

A headscarf or shemagh can help protect your face and neck from the sun, wind, and blowing sand. Additionally, a hat with a brim can provide added protection.

Comfortable Footwear

Choose sturdy, closed-toe shoes or hiking sandals that offer good support and protection against hot sand and rough terrain. Sand can get extremely hot, so you may also want to bring lightweight socks if your shoes allow.

Protective Eyewear

In addition to sunglasses, consider bringing goggles or protective eyewear to shield your eyes from sand and dust when the wind picks up.

First Aid Kit

Pack a basic first aid kit with essentials like bandages, antiseptic wipes, pain relievers, blister pads, and any personal medications you may need.

Navigation Tools

Since the Sahara is a vast desert with minimal signage, consider bringing a map, compass, GPS device, or smartphone with offline maps to navigate the dunes and surrounding areas.

Snacks and Energy Bars

Carry lightweight, non-perishable snacks like nuts, dried fruits, energy bars, or jerky to keep you fueled during long walks or hikes in the desert.

Camera and Accessories

Capture the stunning landscapes and moments of your trip by bringing a camera or smartphone with extra memory cards, batteries, and protective cases to shield your devices from sand and dust.

Remember to pack all these essentials in a sturdy backpack or travel bag that can withstand the desert conditions and keep your belongings organized and easily accessible.

Additionally, consider checking the weather forecast and consulting with local guides or tour operators for any specific recommendations based on your itinerary and activities in the Sahara Desert.
