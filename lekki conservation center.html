Views Of Lekki Conservation Center

About

location:

Lagos, Nigeria

The Lekki Conservation Centre, which is a nature park and wildlife conservation center located in Lagos, Nigeria, was established in 1990 as a way to preserve the natural habitat of the region and to educate people about the importance of conservation.

The park covers an area of 78 hectares and includes a variety of habitats, including a mangrove swamp, freshwater lagoon, and savannah woodland. The highlight of the park is the canopy walkway, which is 330 meters long and 26 meters high. It provides visitors with a unique view of the park and its wildlife.

In addition to the canopy walkway, the park has a nature trail, an aviary, and an insect house. The park is home to a variety of animals, including monkeys, crocodiles, snakes, and birds. It also has a canopy walkway, which is the longest in Africa. The park is open to visitors and offers educational programs and activities.

Things to know before travelling to Lekki Conservation Center

This article is a tip of the iceberg but will equip you with the "Absolute Need to Knows" for Lekki Conservation Center.

How to get there?

The best way to get to the Lekki Conservation Centre is to fly into Murtala Muhammed International Airport in Lagos. From there, you can take a taxi or an airport shuttle to the park. It's about a one-hour drive from the airport to the park.

To get there by car, take the Lekki-Epe Expressway and exit at the Conservation Centre junction. Follow the signs to the park entrance. If you're taking public transportation, you can take a bus or a taxi to the park.

The bus will drop you off at the entrance, and the taxi will take you to the gate. From there, you'll need to walk about 1 km to reach the park. Once you're in Nigeria, the Lekki Conservation Centre is definitely worth a visit! You won't be disappointed.

About the weather

The weather in the Lekki Conservation Centre is tropical, with two distinct seasons: the dry season and the rainy season. The dry season lasts from October to May, and the rainy season lasts from June to September.

During the dry season, the weather is hot and dry, with temperatures reaching up to 35 degrees Celsius. During the rainy season, the weather is hot and humid, with heavy rains and thunderstorms.

The average temperature is around 27 degrees Celsius. It's always a good idea to check the weather forecast before visiting the park, as it can get quite hot and humid.

Medical matters that affect your visit.

Malaria

Malaria is a serious illness that is caused by a parasite and is transmitted by the bite of an infected mosquito. Malaria is common in tropical and subtropical regions, such as Nigeria.

It's important to take anti-malarial medication before and during your visit to the Lekki Conservation Centre, and to take precautions against mosquito bites by wearing long sleeved clothing and long pants, using insect repellent to wade off mosquitoes.

Dehydration

Dehydration can be caused by excessive sweating, diarrhea, or vomiting. It's important to drink plenty of water and to avoid excessive sun exposure, especially during the hot and humid months.

Heat Exhaustion

Heat exhaustion is a serious condition that can occur when the body is exposed to excessive heat and humidity. Symptoms include headache, dizziness, nausea, and fatigue. It's important to seek medical attention if you experience these symptoms.

Snake Bites

Snake bites are rare but can occur in the Lekki Conservation Centre. If you're bitten by a snake, it's important to seek medical attention immediately.

Insect Bites

Insect bites can cause allergic reactions and other health problems. Be sure to use insect repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing to protect yourself from mosquitoes and other insects.

Food And Water Safety

It's important to take precautions when eating and drinking while visiting the Lekki Conservation Centre. Drink only bottled water and avoid eating food that has not been prepared in a sanitary manner.

By taking these precautions, you can help to ensure a safe and enjoyable visit to the Lekki Conservation Centre.

The Reserve Is Located In A Remote Area

The Lekki Conservation Centre is located in a remote area, about 45 minutes from Lagos. This means that it's not as developed or as busy as other tourist destinations. The benefit of this is that it's a true escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. You can really immerse yourself in the natural surroundings and enjoy the peace and tranquility of the reserve.

However, it's important to be aware that the amenities and facilities may not be as developed or as convenient as you're used to. That being said, the staff at the reserve are friendly and helpful, and they'll do their best to make sure you have a great experience.

All in all, the remote location of the Lekki Conservation Centre is part of its charm. So, if you're looking for a place to truly unplug and connect with nature, this is the place for you.

Safety at Lekki Conservation Center

Lekki Conservation Centre in Nigeria is generally considered to be a safe destination for visitors. However, there are a few safety precautions that should be taken into consideration.

First, it is important to be aware of pickpockets and other petty crime, so it is best to keep valuables out of sight and to be mindful of belongings.

In addition, the wildlife in the conservation centre can be unpredictable, so it is important to follow the rules and regulations set by the centre staff. Finally, it is important to be mindful of the weather, as heat stroke and dehydration are possible risks.

Language and communication at Lekki Conservation Center

At the Lekki Conservation Centre, the official language is English, and many of the staff members are fluent in English. However, it is also possible to communicate in Yoruba and Pidgin English, which are the most commonly spoken languages in Lagos State, where the centre is located.

In addition, sign language is often used to communicate with visitors who are deaf or hard of hearing. The centre also has a number of educational materials and exhibits that are available in both English and Yoruba. This makes the centre accessible to a wide range of visitors, regardless of their language ability.

Vital Information on Money Matters

While visiting the Lekki Conservation Centre, it's important to be aware of the money matters, including currency exchange, banking, and tipping.

Currency Exchange

The currency in Nigeria is the Naira. You can exchange foreign currency at banks and authorized currency exchange bureaus.

Banking

There are ATMs available in the area, and most businesses accept major credit cards. However, it's a good idea to have some cash on hand, as not all businesses accept credit cards.

Tipping

It's customary to tip 10-15% for good service in Nigeria. However, be aware that some businesses may add a service charge to the bill, so check before leaving a tip. Also, keep in mind that tipping is not expected at restaurants that already add a service charge to the bill.

When it comes to money matters at the Lekki Conservation Centre, it's important to be aware of the local customs and to plan ahead. With a little bit of preparation, you can ensure that your financial needs are met while visiting this beautiful reserve.

Fun Things To Do At The lekki Conservation Center

There are plenty of fun things to do at the Lekki Conservation Centre, including:

- Take a guided tour of the reserve to learn about the wildlife and plants.

- Go bird watching and see some of the 400+ species of birds that live in the reserve.

- Take a boat ride on the lake to see the wildlife from a different perspective.

- Visit the education center to learn more about conservation and the environment.

- Enjoy a picnic lunch in the park.

- Relax on the beach and soak up the sun.

- Try your hand at kayaking on the lake.

- Go on a night safari to see the nocturnal wildlife.

- Hike through the forest and explore the flora and fauna.

- Try the canopy walkway for a thrilling experience.

- Go fishing in the lake.

- Enjoy a traditional meal at one of the restaurants.

And these are just some of the many things you can do at the Lekki Conservation Centre. Whatever you choose to do, you're sure to have a memorable experience.

Who can travel to Lekki Conservation Center?

The Lekki Conservation Centre is open to everyone, including families, couples, solo travelers, and groups. Children are welcome, and there are even special events for kids.

If you're a nature lover or simply looking for something different to do, the Lekki Conservation Centre is the perfect destination. No matter your age or travel companions, you're sure to find something to enjoy at this unique reserve.

Travel Documents

You will need a valid passport and visa to enter Nigeria. Make sure your passport is valid for at least six months after your travel date and check if you need to obtain a visa before traveling.

What time of the year is best to visit?

The best time to visit the Lekki Conservation Centre is during the dry season, from November to June. This is when the weather is at its best, with temperatures ranging from the low 70s to the high 80s. The rainy season, from July to October, can be a bit more challenging, as it's very hot and humid.

However, if you're a fan of wildlife, this is the time to visit, as you'll have a better chance of seeing some of the migratory species that come to the reserve during this time. No matter when you visit, you're sure to have a great experience at the Lekki conservation center.
