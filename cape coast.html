Views Of Cape coast

About

location:

Southwest of Accra, Ghana

Cape Coast is a historic coastal city located in the Central Region of Ghana, West Africa. It is situated approximately 150 kilometers west of the capital city of Accra. Cape Coast was once the capital of the British Gold Coast and played a significant role in Ghana's colonial history.

The city is famous for its beautiful coastline and historical landmarks. The most prominent of these landmarks is Cape Coast Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in the 17th century by European traders, the castle was used for the trans-Atlantic slave trade.

Today, it serves as a museum and memorial, providing visitors with a glimpse into the dark history of the slave trade. It is a powerful and emotionally charged site that attracts tourists from all over the world.

Aside from Cape Coast Castle, the city also boasts several other historical sites such as the Elmina Castle, Fort William, and Fort Victoria. These fortresses were also used in the slave trade era and are now open to the public.

Cape Coast is known for its vibrant culture and traditional festivals. One of the most popular is the Fetu Afahye festival, celebrated by the people of Cape Coast in honor of their ancestors. It is a colorful and joyous event that features processions, traditional music and dance, drumming, and masquerades.

The city has a rich educational heritage as well. Cape Coast is home to the University of Cape Coast, one of the leading universities in Ghana. The university has a strong focus on education and offers various academic programs.

In terms of natural beauty, Cape Coast offers stunning beaches along its coastline, such as the Victoria Beach and the Oasis Beach. These are popular destinations for both locals and tourists, providing opportunities for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Cape Coast also has a bustling fishing industry, and visitors can enjoy fresh seafood at the local restaurants. The city has a lively market where you can find a variety of local products, crafts, and souvenirs.

Overall, Cape Coast is a city that combines history, culture, and natural beauty. It affords visitors the chance to learn about Ghana's past, explore historical landmarks, and enjoy the beautiful beaches and vibrant local culture.

Things to know before travelling to Cape coast

This article is a tip of the iceberg but will equip you with the "Absolute Need to Knows" for Cape coast.

How to get there?

If you're coming to Cape Coast from outside of Ghana, the best option is to fly into Kotoka International Airport in Accra, the capital city. From Accra, you can take a bus, taxi, or minibus to Cape Coast. The journey takes about 2-3 hours. Another option is to fly into Takoradi Airport, which is closer to Cape Coast, but it offers fewer flight options.

If you're coming from Takoradi, another major city in Ghana, you can take a bus or minibus to Cape Coast. The journey from Takoradi takes about 1-2 hours. The airport in Takoradi also offers flights to Cape Coast and other destinations in Ghana.

About the weather

The weather in Cape Coast, Ghana, is influenced by its location in the tropical region of West Africa. It experiences a tropical savanna climate, characterized by distinct wet and dry seasons.

Cape Coast has two main seasons: the rainy season, which occurs between April and July, and the dry season, which extends from November to March. The transitional months of October and November and the transitional period in March and April often experience some rainfall as well.

During the rainy season, Cape Coast receives heavy rainfall and occasional thunderstorms. The average monthly rainfall ranges between 100-200 mm (4-8 inches), with June often being the wettest month. The temperatures during this period are slightly lower, ranging between 24°C (75°F) and 28°C (82°F).

In contrast, the dry season in Cape Coast is characterized by warm temperatures and lower humidity. Average temperatures range from 28°C (82°F) to 32°C (90°F), with January and February being the hottest months. The dry season is also associated with harmattan winds, which blow dusty air from the Sahara Desert and can cause a decrease in visibility.

Overall, Cape Coast enjoys a tropical climate with warm temperatures year-round. However, it's important to note that weather patterns can vary from year to year, and this information provides a general overview of the weather in Cape Coast, Ghana.

Medical matters that affect your visit.

When visiting Cape Coast, like any other destination, there are several medical matters that can affect your trip. These matters can be related to pre-existing medical conditions, general health precautions, or specific health risks associated with the location. Here are some medical matters to consider:

Vaccinations

Before traveling to Cape Coast, it is recommended to ensure that your routine vaccinations are up to date. Additionally, certain vaccines, such as hepatitis A and typhoid, may be necessary. You should consult with a healthcare professional or visit a travel clinic to receive any required vaccinations.

Malaria

Cape Coast is located in a region where malaria is prevalent. It is important to take appropriate measures to prevent bites from mosquitoes, such as using insect repellents, wearing protective clothing, and sleeping under mosquito nets or in air-conditioned rooms. It is also advisable to take antimalarial medication as prescribed by your doctor.

Food and water safety

Pay attention to the quality of food and water you consume while in Cape Coast. Drink only bottled water or properly treated water, and avoid consuming raw or undercooked foods. Eating at reputable establishments and practicing good hygiene, such as washing your hands regularly, can help prevent foodborne illnesses.

Sun protection

Cape Coast experiences a tropical climate, and exposure to strong sunlight can lead to sunburn or heat-related illnesses. Wear protective clothing, use sunscreen with a high SPF, and seek shade during peak sun hours.

Respiratory illnesses

Like many crowded destinations, Cape Coast may have increased risk of respiratory infections such as the common cold or flu. Practice good respiratory hygiene, such as covering your mouth when coughing or sneezing, and frequently washing your hands to reduce the risk of transmission.

Pre-existing conditions

If you have any pre-existing medical conditions, ensure you have an adequate supply of necessary medications for the duration of your trip. It's a good idea to carry a copy of your medical history, prescriptions, and contact details of your healthcare providers in case of emergencies.

While this list is not exhaustive, paying attention to these medical matters can help ensure a safe and healthy visit to Cape Coast.

It is always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional or a travel clinic before traveling to any destination to receive personalized advice based on your specific health needs.

Clothing and culture

In Cape Coast, it's important to dress modestly and respectfully. Men should wear shirts with sleeves and long pants, and women should wear skirts or dresses that cover their knees and shoulders.

Additionally, it's a good idea to bring a light scarf to cover your head when entering places of worship, such as churches and mosques.

It's also important to be aware of the importance of greetings in Cape Coast. When you meet someone, it's customary to greet them with a handshake, a smile, and the phrase "Akwaaba," which means "welcome" in the local language.

Finally, it's considered impolite to rush someone when they are talking, so be prepared for long conversations and plenty of small talk.

Safety at Cape coast

Cape Coast is generally considered a safe destination for travelers, but there are a few safety precautions to keep in mind. First, it's important to be aware of pickpocketing, which is the most common crime in the city.

Make sure to keep your valuables in a secure place, like a money belt or cross-body bag, and be aware of your surroundings when using ATMs or withdrawing cash. Additionally, women should avoid walking alone at night, and everyone should avoid traveling alone in isolated areas. It's also a good idea to register with your embassy upon arrival in Cape Coast.

Language and communication at Cape coast

Cape Coast is home to a variety of languages, including English, Twi, Fanti, and Ga. However, English is the most commonly spoken language, and most people in the city speak at least some English.

If you don't speak English, you may want to consider bringing along a phrasebook or translation app to help you communicate. Additionally, body language is an important part of communication in Cape Coast.

Vital information on money matters

There are a few important things to know about money matters in Cape Coast. First, the currency is the Ghanaian cedi (GHS). You can exchange US dollars, euros, and British pounds at banks and currency exchange offices.

ATMs are available in the city, and most major credit cards are accepted in larger hotels and restaurants. However, it's best to have cash on hand for small purchases and tipping. It's also a good idea to carry small bills, as it can be difficult to get change for large bills.

Fun things to do at Cape coast

Cape Coast is a historic coastal city in Ghana that offers a range of fun activities and experiences. Here are some fun things to do in Cape Coast:

Visit Cape coast castle

One of the most significant historical sites in Ghana, Cape Coast Castle is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Explore the castle's dungeons, walk through the "Door of No Return," and learn about the transatlantic slave trade.

Relax on Cape coast beaches

Cape Coast is known for its beautiful beaches like Brenu Beach and Oasis Beach. Enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and walking along the shoreline. You can also rent a beach hut or try some local beachside cuisine.

Kakum National Park Canopy Walkway

This exhilarating experience allows you to walk among the treetops of the rainforest on suspension bridges and platforms. Enjoy breathtaking views, spot wildlife, and learn about the tropical ecosystem.

Attend a Traditional Festival

Cape Coast is rich in cultural heritage, and there are several vibrant festivals throughout the year. The Fetu Afahye Festival, held in September, is one of the most famous where locals celebrate their ancestors with music, dancing, and elaborate processions.

Explore Elmina Castle

Located near Cape Coast, Elmina Castle is another historic fortification and UNESCO World Heritage Site. Take a guided tour to learn about its fascinating history and see the impressive architecture.

Learn Drumming and Dancing

Engage in a cultural experience by participating in traditional drumming and dancing workshops. Talented instructors will teach you the rhythms and dance moves unique to the region.

Batik and Pottery Workshops

Discover the traditional Ghanaian art of batik-making and pottery. Take part in workshops where you can learn these crafts and create your own unique designs.

Taste Local Cuisine

Ghanaian cuisine offers a delightful array of flavors. Explore the local food scene in Cape Coast by indulging in dishes like waakye, fufu with groundnut soup, banku, and fresh seafood.

Take a Boat Ride on the Oguaa River

Enjoy a relaxing boat ride along the Oguaa River, explore the mangrove swamps, and spot various bird species. You can also learn about local fishing practices and enjoy the serene natural surroundings.

Visit the Cape Coast Cultural Center

Learn more about Ghana's cultural heritage by visiting the Cape Coast Cultural Center. It houses several exhibitions showcasing traditional art, artifacts, and costumes. These are just a few of the exciting things to do in Cape Coast, Ghana. The city is filled with history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture, making it a fantastic destination for exploration and adventure.

Who can travel to Cape coast?

Cape Coast is a destination that can be enjoyed by all types of travelers, from solo travelers to families and groups. The city is relatively small and easy to navigate, and there are plenty of activities to keep everyone entertained.

That said, it's important to note that Ghana is a developing country, and there may be some challenges for those with physical disabilities. Additionally, while the majority of people in Cape Coast are friendly and welcoming, some cultural differences may be experienced.

Overall, Cape Coast is a great destination for all types of travelers, as long as they are prepared to be flexible and open-minded.

Travel Documents

You will need a valid passport and visa to enter Ghana. Make sure your passport is valid for at least six months after your travel date and check if you need to obtain a visa before traveling.

What time of the year is best to visit?

The best time to visit Cape Coast is from December to March. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is calm, making it a great time to enjoy the beach. Additionally, December is when the biggest festival in Cape Coast takes place - the Fetu Afahye festival, which celebrates the city's history and culture.

If you're interested in experiencing this festival, make sure to book your trip early, as the city can get quite busy during this time. April to June is also a great time to visit, as the weather is still pleasant and there are fewer tourists.

Lightweight and breathable clothing

Swimwear

Cape Coast has beautiful beaches, so don't forget to pack your swimwear.

Light rain jacket or umbrella

Be prepared for unpredictable tropical rain showers.

Hat and sunglasses

Sunscreen

Protect your skin from the strong tropical sun.

Basic first aid kit

Mobile phone and charger

Portable power bank

Travel insurance documents

Money and debit/credit cards
