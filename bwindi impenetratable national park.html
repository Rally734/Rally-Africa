Views Of Bwindi Impenetrable National Park

About

location:

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park, Uganda

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in southwestern Uganda. It is best known for being home to almost half of the world's population of mountain gorillas, making it a popular destination for gorilla trekking.

The park covers an area of around 331 square kilometers and is characterized by its dense forests, steep hills, and diverse wildlife. In addition to mountain gorillas, Bwindi is also home to other primate species such as chimpanzees, baboons, and colobus monkeys, as well as a variety of bird species.

Visitors to Bwindi have the opportunity to go gorilla trekking, which involves hiking through the park to observe and interact with mountain gorillas in their natural habitat. This experience is highly regulated to ensure the safety of both the gorillas and the visitors.

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is not only a critical conservation area for mountain gorillas but also plays a vital role in supporting the local community through ecotourism initiatives. It is a must-visit destination for nature and wildlife enthusiasts looking to experience one of the most unique and captivating wildlife encounters in the world.

Things to know before travelling to Bwindi Impenetrable National Park

This article is a tip of the iceberg but will equip you with the "Absolute Need to Knows" for Bwindi Impenetrable National Park .

How to get there?

To get to Bwindi Impenetrable National Park, you'll need to fly into Entebbe International Airport in Uganda. From there, you can take a domestic flight to Kihihi or Kisoro, which are both close to the park. Once you arrive, you can arrange for a private car or take a bus to the park entrance.

The roads to the park can be rough, so it's best to allow plenty of time for the journey. Alternatively, you can book a tour that includes transport to the park, which will take care of all the logistics for you.

About the weather

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is located in a mountainous region, so the weather can be variable. The park is generally warm year-round, with temperatures ranging from the high 60s to the high 80s Fahrenheit. However, the weather can be unpredictable, and it's not uncommon for it to rain at any time of year.

The rainy season is from March to May and from September to November, and the dry season is from December to February and from June to August. If you're planning a visit, it's best to pack for a range of weather conditions.

Medical matters that affect your visit

When planning a visit to Bwindi Impenetrable National Park in Uganda, it's important to consider various medical matters to ensure your health and safety during your trip. Here are some medical considerations that may affect your visit to the park:

Yellow Fever Vaccination

Uganda requires proof of yellow fever vaccination for travelers coming from countries with risk of yellow fever transmission. This is important to prevent the spread of the disease and to protect public health.

Malaria

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is located in a malaria-endemic region. It is essential to take appropriate measures to prevent malaria during your visit, such as taking antimalarial medication, using insect repellent, and sleeping under mosquito nets.

Medical Facilities

While there may be some basic medical facilities in nearby towns, the availability of comprehensive healthcare services may be limited in remote areas like Bwindi. It's advised to carry a basic first aid kit and any essential medications you may need during your stay.

Hiking and Physical Fitness

Gorilla trekking, a popular activity in Bwindi Impenetrable National Park, involves hiking through the dense forest to track mountain gorillas. It's important to be in good physical condition to participate in these activities, as the terrain can be challenging.

Altitude Sickness

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is situated at varying altitudes, and some visitors may experience symptoms of altitude sickness, especially if coming from lower elevations. It's recommended to acclimatize slowly, stay hydrated, and seek medical help if symptoms worsen.

Food and Water Safety

Ensure that you drink only bottled or purified water and eat food from reputable sources to avoid gastrointestinal illnesses. It's advisable to practice good hygiene and hand washing to prevent infections.

Travel Insurance

Consider purchasing travel insurance that covers medical emergencies, evacuation, and repatriation in case of unexpected health issues during your visit to Bwindi Impenetrable National Park.

It's always a good idea to consult with a healthcare provider or travel medicine specialist before your trip to receive personalized medical advice and recommendations based on your individual health profile and travel plans.

Is it safe around the Gorillas?

Mountain Gorillas in Uganda are not considered dangerous as long as everything is peaceful and relaxed. The large male mountain gorillas or silverbacks are generally gentle and shy by nature. The gorilla groups have become habitual of the human presence amidst them. However there are certain set of rules that needs to be followed without fail, for the sake of your and your group's safety.

Here are some of the rules for gorilla trekking:

. A minimum distance of 6-10 ft needs to be kept between you and the primates.

. A mask is mandatory if you have come from a high risk Covid country.

. If the silverbacks approaches or charge at you aggressively, you need to stay calm. Do not think of running away, rather go into a submissive position and do not lock eyes with the giant male.

.Do not make sudden moves or loud noises.

.Locking eyes with the apes are seen as an act of hostility and can cause the ape to go berserk.

.No smoking, drinking or eating is allowed wihin a vicinity of 200 meters of a gorilla troop.

.Children below the age of 15 are not allowed to participate in the trek.

Are taking pictures allowed?

Taking pictures around the gorillas is allowed, but keep in mind that the flash of your camera or phone is off. The sudden burst of light might agitate and scare the group, resulting the alpha male breaking into a fit of rage.

The reason being is that the silverback is the protector of the gorilla family and would treat any form of sudden threat with extreme hostility. Also make sure to carry a waterproof bag for your camera, to protect the camera from the rains.

How to get your permits?

Travellers planning a Mountain gorilla trekking can get their permits online. Be sure to book them in advance as they often sell out well in advance. If you are visitng Bwindi National Park the local rangers strongly recommend visitors book their gorilla permits through a safari agent or a local tour operator.

The reason behind this is that the roads of Bwindi can be a bit treacheous and challenging, if not approached the right way.

How fit do you need to be?

It is not certain for how long would you have to trek before reaching the spot where the gorilla family is located. The level of fitness of the entire group is what decides which trek the group will take.

Trekking to the spot where the gorillas put up often requires athletic levels of fitness. Mountain gorilla tours require you to be in your physical state as you can never be sure which group you get paired along with.

Vital information on money matters

The official currency of Uganda is the Ugandan shilling, and it's best to carry some cash with you when you visit Bwindi Impenetrable National Park. There are no ATMs in the park, and most businesses only accept cash. Credit cards are not widely accepted, so it's best to plan ahead and exchange your currency before you arrive.

There are a few places to exchange money in the nearby towns, but rates can be variable, so it's best to compare offers before exchanging. Also, be aware that many places will not accept older, torn, or damaged bills.

Fun things to do at Bwindi Impenetrable National Park

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park in Uganda is a remarkable destination known for its population of endangered mountain gorillas. Here are some fun things to do at Bwindi Impenetrable National Park:

Gorilla Trekking

One of the main attractions at Bwindi is the opportunity to go gorilla trekking and observe these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat. This activity requires a permit and advance booking but is a memorable experience for any nature enthusiast.

Bird Watching

Bwindi is a haven for bird lovers, boasting over 350 bird species. Bird watching tours can be organized to spot colorful and rare birds in the park's diverse habitats.

Nature Walks and Hiking

Explore the lush rainforest on guided nature walks and hiking trails within the park. You can discover the park's unique flora and fauna, waterfalls, and scenic viewpoints.

Cultural Encounters

Engage with the local communities surrounding Bwindi and learn about their traditional ways of life. Cultural experiences may include visits to villages, craft centers, and cultural performances.

Batwa Trail Experience

Join a guided Batwa Trail to learn about the indigenous Batwa people, their forest-dwelling lifestyle, and traditional skills. This interactive experience provides insights into the culture and history of the Batwa community.

Canopy Walk

Experience the thrill of walking on a canopy walkway suspended high above the forest floor. The canopy walk offers panoramic views of the forest and a unique perspective of the park's biodiversity.

Photography

Bwindi offers stunning natural scenery and wildlife, making it a paradise for photography enthusiasts. Capture the beauty of the landscapes, gorillas, birds, and other wildlife species during your visit.

Relaxation and Wellness

Unwind and relax in the tranquil setting of Bwindi by enjoying spa treatments, yoga sessions, or simply taking time to rejuvenate amidst nature's beauty.

Remember to follow park regulations, respect the wildlife, and prioritize conservation efforts during your visit to Bwindi Impenetrable National Park.

Who can travel to Bwindi Impenetrable National Park?

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is open to all ages, and there are no restrictions on who can visit. However, the park is located in a mountainous area, and some of the hikes are quite strenuous. The Gorilla Trek is especially challenging, so it's not recommended for those with mobility issues or young children.

For those who are not able to hike, there are other activities available in the park, such as birdwatching and nature walks. Whatever your ability level, there is something for everyone at Bwindi Impenetrable National Park!

Travel Documents

You will need a valid passport and visa to enter Uganda. Make sure your passport is valid for at least six months after your travel date and check if you need to obtain a visa before traveling.

What time of the year is best to visit?

While Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is beautiful year-round, the best time to visit is during the dry season. From June to August and December to February, the weather is generally sunny and mild, making it a perfect time to spot wildlife and enjoy the scenery.

During the rainy season, the park is lush and green, but it can be muddy and slippery. It's also important to note that gorilla trekking is only available during the dry season, so plan accordingly if you hope to see these amazing creatures.

Packing essentials for your trip

Here are some packing essentials for a trip to Bwindi Impenetrable National Park in Uganda:

Clothing

Pack lightweight, breathable clothing suitable for hot and humid weather. Include long-sleeved shirts and pants to protect against mosquitoes and the sun. Also, bring a waterproof jacket or poncho for potential rain showers.

Footwear

Sturdy, comfortable walking shoes or hiking boots are essential for trekking through the rugged terrain of Bwindi Impenetrable National Park. Make sure they are broken in before your trip to avoid blisters

Insect Repellent

Mosquitoes and other insects are prevalent in the park, so pack insect repellent containing DEET to protect yourself from bites and potential diseases like malaria.

Sun Protection

Don't forget to pack sunscreen with a high SPF, sunglasses, and a hat to protect yourself from the strong African sun during your hikes.

Camera or Binoculars

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is known for its diverse wildlife, including mountain gorillas. Bring a good camera or binoculars to capture these amazing creatures and other wildlife you may encounter.

Water bottle

Staying hydrated is important, especially during trekking activities. Carry a reusable water bottle to fill up along the way and reduce plastic waste.

Travel Documents

Don't forget to pack your passport, travel insurance information, permits for gorilla trekking (if applicable), and any other important travel documents.

First-aid kit

Bring a basic first aid kit with essentials like bandages, antiseptic wipes, pain relievers, and any personal medications you may need.

Snacks

Pack some energy-boosting snacks like nuts, granola bars, and dried fruits to keep you going during long treks.

Cash

While some establishments may accept credit cards, it's always a good idea to have some cash on hand for small purchases or tipping guides and drivers.

Other essentials include:

.A rain jacket as it may rain during the trek

.Long and thick socks to keep the creepy crawlers away from your legs

.Gaiters to use in those muddy swamp patches

.Do not wear blue or black clothing as it attracts tsetse flies. Also, camouflage or military clothing is only for African military personnel.

.A tough pair of gloves, for people who like to grab stuff

.A walking stick that you can buy at the park gate

.A rucksack to carry all your trekking essentials and some snacks

Remember to pack light and only bring essentials to make your trip to Bwindi Impenetrable National Park more comfortable and enjoyable.
