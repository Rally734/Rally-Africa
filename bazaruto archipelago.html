Views Of Bazaruto Archiepelago

About

location:

Inhambane Province, Mozambique

The Bazaruto Archipelago consists of six islands: Bazaruto, Benguerra, Magaruque, Santa Carolina, Bangue, and Pansy Shell, off the coast of Mozambique, in the Indian Ocean. Of these, Bazaruto and Benguerra are the most popular and have the most tourist facilities. Bazaruto island is the largest island in the archipelago, and it's known for it's sandy beaches, lush vegetation, and diverse wildlife.

Benguerra island is smaller, but it has an equally beautiful landscape and is also known for its sandy beaches and turquoise waters. Both islands have a range of accomodation options, from luxury resorts to more budget-friendly options. There are also several small villages on both islands, where you can get a glimpse of local life.

If you want to explore more of the archipelago, you can take a boat tour to the smaller islands, such as Magaruque and Santa Carolina. These islands are less developed and offer a more rustic and authentic experience.

The islands are known for their crystal-clear water, white sand beaches and home to diverse range of wildlife, including Dugongs, Turtles, Dolphins, and Humpback Whales.

Things to know before travelling to Bazaruto Archipelago

This article is a tip of the iceberg but will equip you with the "Absolute Need to Knows" for Bazaruto Archipelago.

How to get there?

There are a few different ways to get to the Bazaruto Archipelago. The easiest way is to fly into Vilankulo Airport, which is located on the mainland, about 45 minutes away from the islands. From there you can take a speedboat or a traditional dhow to the islands.

There are also several charter flights that operate between Maputo International Airport and the islands. Another option is to take a bus or minibus from Maputo to Vilankulo, and then take a boat from there.

About the weather

The weather in the Bazaruto Archipelago is tropical and warm year-round. The average temperature ranges from 20 degrees Celsius(68 degrees Fahrenheit) to 30 degrees Celsius(86 degrees Fahrenheit), with the hottest months being October and April. The rainy season is from November to March, when there is a chance of heavy rainfall and thunderstorms, which can lead to flooding in some areas.

The driest months are May to October, when the skies are clear and humidity is low. However, the winds can be quite strong during this time. It's important to note that the weather can vary from island to island.

Medical matters that affect your visit.

There are a few medical matters that you should be aware of before visiting the Bazaruto Archipelago. Firstly, the archipelago is in a malaria-endemic area, so it's important to take preventative measures against malaria, such as taking anti-malaria medication and using mosquito repellent.

Secondly, the archipelago is also home to a number of venomous animals, such as Snakes and Spiders, so it's important to be aware of the risks and take appropriate precautions.

Lastly, it's important to be aware of the heat and humidity in the archipelago, as it can lead to heat exhaustion and dehydration. It's important to stay hydrated and avoid sun exposure during the hottest part of the day. By taking these precautions, you can ensure a safe and healthy visit to the Bazaruto Archipelago.

And if you do need medical assistance while you're there, there is a small clinic on Bazaruto island. However, for more serious medical issues, you may need to be evacuated to Maputo or another city in Mozambique. But don't let that worry you - just focus on enjoying your trip!

About the cyclones in Bazaruto Archipelago

The Bazaruto Archipelago is located in an area that is prone to cyclones, which are tropical storms that can cause severe damage and flooding. The cyclone season in the archipelago typically runs from November to April, with the peak season being between January and March.

During this time, there is a risk of strong winds, heavy rainfall, and storm surges. If you're planning to visit during this time, it's important to be aware of the potential risks and be prepared for any disruptions to your travel plans. The best way to stay safe during a cyclone is to follow the instructions of the local authorities.

Language and communication at Bazaruto Archipelago

The Bazaruto Archipelago is located off the coast of Mozambique, and the main language spoken in the area is Portuguese. Many of the locals also speak English, and there are a number of tour operators and hotels that cater to English-speaking guests.

For those who do not speak Portuguese or English, there are a few hotels that offer translation services. Additionally, many of the locals speak Tsonga, which is a Bantu language that is widely spoken in southern Africa.

Vital information on money matters.

The local currency in the Bazaruto Archipelago is the Mozambican metical(MZN). It's a good idea to bring some cash in US dollars or South African rand, as ATMs are not always reliable and credit cards are not widely accepted.

There are a few banks and currency exchange offices on the main island of Bazaruto, so you can easily exchange your money once you arrive.

Fun things to do at the Bazaruto Archipelago

The Bazaruto Archipelago is not just a great destination for fun activities, but it's also a place to reconnect with nature and enjoy the simple pleasures of life. Here are some of the fun things to do at the Bazaruto Archipelago:

Snorkeling and scuba diving

The archipelago is home to some of the most pristine coral reefs in the world, and the clear, warm waters are perfect for snorkeling and diving. You can see a variety of colorful fish, coral, and even some dolphins and turtles!

Dhow cruises

A dhow is a traditional sailing boat, and taking a dhow cruise around the archipelago is a unique and relaxing experience. You can explore the islands from the water, taking in stunning views and spotting wildlife like dolphins and birds. Plus, you can stop for a swim in the crystal-clear waters.

Fishing

The waters around the archipelago are known for their abundance of fish, including Marlin, Tuna, and Barracuda. Many resorts offer fish charters, and you can even cook your catch on the beach.

Wildlife safaris

The Bazaruto Archipelago is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including Elephants, Buffalo, Hippos, Lions and Leopards. Many of the resorts offer safari tours, which are a great way to see animals in their natural habitat.

Relaxing on the beach

If you're looking for a relaxing vacation, the Bazaruto Archipelago is the perfect destination. With its soft, white sand and azure waters, it's easy to find a quiet place and soak up the sun. Plus, the resorts offer a variety of amenities, like spa treatments, yoga classes, and delicious food. You'll feel refreshed and rejuvenated after a stay on the archipelago!

Who can travel to Bazaruto Archipelago?

The Bazaruto Archipelago is a popular tourist destination that is open to visitors from all over the world. There are no restrictions based on nationality, age, or gender. The main requirement for visitors is that they must have a valid passport and visa, if required.

Additionally, some of the resorts in the archipelago have age restrictions for guests. For example, some resorts are adults-only, while others are open to all ages. Visitors should also be aware of the current travel advisories for Mozambique, as there may be safety concerns in the area.

Travel Documents

You will need a valid passport and visa to enter Mozambique. Make sure your passport is valid for at least six months after your travel date and check if you need to obtain a visa before traveling.

What time of the year is best to visit?

The best time to visit the Bazaruto Archipelago is during the dry season, from May to October. This is when the weather is the most pleasant, with warm temperatures and clear skies. The days are generally sunny and the nights are cool and refreshing.

It's also the best time for wildlife viewing, as the animals are more active and the vegetation is less dense. Plus, there is less chance of cyclones or heavy rains disrupting your trip. So if you're planning a trip to the archipelago, we'd recommend visiting during the dry season.

With these items packed, you'll be ready to experience the beauty and tranquility of the Bazaruto Archipelago. Now, all you need to do is pack your suitcase and get ready for the adventure of a lifetime!
